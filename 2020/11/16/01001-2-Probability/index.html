Posted on 2020-11-16 Edited on 2020-11-23 In 01001 Views:

Probability

Types:

  • data-based probability
  • model-based probability

Probability results

Classical probablity

with sample space $\Omega$ event A,

$P(\Omega)=1$

  • complement
    $P(A`)=1-P(A)$

  • mutually exclusive
    $P(A\cap B)=0$
    $P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)$

  • independence
    $P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)$
    $P(A|B)=P(A)$

  • union rule
    $P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)$

  • conditional probility
    $P(A|B)=\frac{P(A\cap B)}{P(B)}$

  • Multiplication rule
    $P(A\cap B) = P(A|B)P(B)$
    Joint probability = conditional probability     P(condition)

  • Bayes theoren
    $P(A|B)=\frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}$

  • the law of total probabilitiy
    $P(A)=\sum_iP(A|B_i)(B_i)$

Random variable

Distribution

-permutation
$nPr=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$

  • combination
    $(_{x}^{n})=\frac{n!}{x!(n-x)!}$

    Probability functions

  • for discrete variables:
    probability distribution function: $P (X = x)$
  • for continuous variables:
    probability density function (pdf) : $f(x)$
  • for both discrete and continuous variables:
    cumulative distribution function (CDF): $F(x)=P(X ≤ x)$
